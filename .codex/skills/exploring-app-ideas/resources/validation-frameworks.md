## Core Validation Principles

### The Build-Measure-Learn Loop

```
IDEA
  ↓
BUILD (Minimum Viable Test)
  ↓
MEASURE (Collect Data)
  ↓
LEARN (Insights)
  ↓
PIVOT or PERSEVERE
  ↓
(repeat)
```

**Key principle**: Validate assumptions with minimum effort before building.

### Hierarchy of Evidence

**Strongest → Weakest**:

1. ✅ **Paying customers** (they gave you money)
2. ✅ **Active users** (they use product regularly)
3. ⚠️ **Email signups** (they showed interest)
4. ⚠️ **Survey responses** (they said they'd use it)
5. ⚠️ **Positive interviews** (they validated problem)
6. ❌ **You think it's a good idea** (zero evidence)

**Goal**: Move up the hierarchy before investing months in development.

## 3. Unique Value Proposition

**High-Concept Pitch** (one sentence):
[The simplest, clearest explanation of why your app is different and valuable]

**Examples**:
- Uber: "Tap a button, get a ride"
- Airbnb: "Belong anywhere"
- Notion: "One workspace for your notes, tasks, and wikis"

**Your UVP**:
```
For [target customer]
Who [statement of need or opportunity]
Our [product/service name]
Is a [product category]
That [statement of key benefit]

Unlike [primary competitive alternative]
Our product [statement of primary differentiation]

```

Who waste time context-switching between tools

Is an all-in-one workspace
That keeps all project context in one place

Unlike Notion and Jira

```

  - [All features + support]

- ARPU: $[Average revenue per user]
- MRR: $[Monthly recurring revenue]
- ARR: $[Annual recurring revenue]
```

**Lifetime Value (LTV)**:
```
ARPU: $[X]/month
Retention: [Y] months average
LTV = $[X] × [Y] = $[Z]
```

## 7. Cost Structure

**Fixed Costs** (regardless of users):
- Development: $[X]/month (team salaries)
- Infrastructure base: $[X]/month (hosting minimums)
- Tools & software: $[X]/month (Figma, GitHub, etc)
- Office / admin: $[X]/month

**Variable Costs** (per user/transaction):
- Hosting: $[X] per 1000 users
- Third-party APIs: $[X] per transaction
- Payment processing: [X]% of revenue
- Customer support: $[X] per ticket

**Cost to Acquire Customer (CAC)**:
```
Marketing spend: $[X]/month
Sales team: $[Y]/month
New customers: [Z]/month

CAC = ($[X] + $[Y]) / [Z] = $[Result]
```

**Unit Economics**:
```
LTV: $[A]
CAC: $[B]
LTV:CAC = [A/B] (target: > 3)

Payback period: [B] / ([A] / [retention months]) = [X] months (target: < 12)
```

### How to Use Lean Canvas

**Step-by-step process**:

1. **Start with Problem** (20 min)
   - List top 3 problems
   - Identify existing alternatives
   - Define customer segments

2. **Define Solution** (20 min)
   - List top 3 features (minimum)
   - Map features to problems
   - Cut everything non-essential

3. **Unique Value Proposition** (30 min)
   - Write high-concept pitch
   - Craft differentiation statement
   - Test with 5 people for clarity

4. **Channels & Revenue** (20 min)
   - Pick 1-2 acquisition channels
   - Define pricing model
   - Calculate unit economics

5. **Metrics & Costs** (20 min)
   - Choose OMTM
   - Estimate costs
   - Set success criteria

**Iteration**:
- Update weekly as you learn
- Version control (v1, v2, v3...)
- Compare versions to track pivots

## Value Proposition Canvas

### Customer Profile

**Customer Jobs**:
```markdown
## Functional Jobs
What tasks are customers trying to complete?

1. [Job 1]: [Description]
   - Context: [When/where]
   - Success: [What "done" looks like]
   - Importance: [1-10]

2. [Job 2]

## Social Jobs
How do customers want to be perceived?

1. [Job 1]: [e.g., Look productive to manager]
2. [Job 2]: [e.g., Be seen as expert]

## Emotional Jobs
How do customers want to feel?

1. [Job 1]: [e.g., Feel in control]
2. [Job 2]: [e.g., Reduce stress]
```

**Customer Pains**:
```markdown
## Obstacles
What prevents customers from getting jobs done?

1. [Pain 1]: [Description]
   - Severity: [1-10]
   - Frequency: [How often]

## Risks
What could go wrong or have negative consequences?

1. [Risk 1]: [e.g., Data loss]
2. [Risk 2]: [e.g., Wasted time]

## Undesired outcomes
What are unwanted results or characteristics?

1. [Outcome 1]: [e.g., Too complex]
2. [Outcome 2]: [e.g., Expensive]
```

**Customer Gains**:
```markdown
## Required gains
Without these, the solution won't work

1. [Gain 1]: [e.g., Must save time]
2. [Gain 2]: [e.g., Must be reliable]

## Expected gains
Standard expectations

1. [Gain 1]: [e.g., Easy to use]
2. [Gain 2]: [e.g., Good support]

## Desired gains
Exceed expectations

1. [Gain 1]: [e.g., Delightful UX]
2. [Gain 2]: [e.g., Surprising features]

## Unexpected gains
Go beyond what customers imagine

1. [Gain 1]: [e.g., AI does work for them]
```

### Value Map

**Products & Services**:
```markdown
1. [Core product]
   - [Key feature 1]
   - [Key feature 2]

2. [Additional service]
```

**Pain Relievers**:
```markdown
How do your products/services alleviate pains?

1. [Feature/benefit] reduces [specific pain]
2. [Feature/benefit] eliminates [obstacle]
3. [Feature/benefit] mitigates [risk]

Map each pain reliever to a specific customer pain.
```

**Gain Creators**:
```markdown
How do your products/services create gains?

1. [Feature/benefit] delivers [required gain]
2. [Feature/benefit] exceeds [expected gain]
3. [Feature/benefit] surprises with [unexpected gain]

Map each gain creator to a specific customer gain.
```

### Fit Assessment

**Questions**:
- [ ] Do pain relievers address the most extreme pains?
- [ ] Do gain creators deliver required and expected gains?
- [ ] Are there gains/pains you haven't addressed? (okay to ignore some)
- [ ] Does value proposition differentiate from alternatives?

**Ranking pains and gains**:
```
Extreme (10): Can't live without solving
Significant (7-9): Very important
Moderate (4-6): Nice to have
Minor (1-3): Don't care much
```

Focus on extreme and significant pains first.
